Who Funds Undocumented New Yorkers?

Government services exclude an estimated 560,000 undocumented New Yorkers every year. In immigrant communities, local residents are collaborating to meet the needs of their neighbors.

By Sara Herschander

October 11, 2020

As New York City reopens, its mutual aid networks are passing from crisis mode into a new phase of sustained community support. Groups that originated for emergency food and medicine deliveries are now confronting entrenched community challenges that precede the pandemic, like unemployment, food insecutiy, and rising rents.

In the neighboring communities of Elmhurst and Corona, once called 'the epicenter of the epicenter' of the global COVID-19 crisis, these mutual aid efforts are especially important. The area is home to the highest rate of foreign-born residents in the city and many are not eligible for government assistance nor unemployment benefits. In Elmhurst and Corona, where one in four residents lives under the poverty line, mutual aid networks are trying to fill this enormous gap in government services, despite limited resources and a high level of need.

INCOME

In Elmhurst and Corona, 52.3% of families live below or near the poverty level, yet only 2% of residents receive Cash Assistance from NY State. Undocumented New Yorkers are ineligible for financial assistance programs like Supplemental Security Income (SSI) and Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF). Nor are they eligible for NY unemployment insurance. During the COVID-19 crisis, undocumented New Yorkers were excluded from direct economic assistance through the federal CARES Act. In April, NYC established a $20 million fund for up to 20,000 undocumented workers and their families.

Community Financial Assistance: Chhaya CDC | Centro Corona Mutual Aid Network | Immigrant Families Fund | One Fair Wage Emergency Fund | Service Workers Coalition | All available regardless of immigration status.

FOOD

The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP) covers one in five households in Elmhurst and Corona, though undocumented residents are ineligible. Undocumented residents are also ineligible for the Women, Infants, and Children (WIC) Program.

Community Food Assistance: Get Food NYC provides listings for local Grab & Go Meals at NYC Public Schools and food pantries, including 11 in Elmhurst/Corona | Elmhurst Community Fridge 2 | All available regardless of immigration status.

HOUSING

In Elmhurst and Corona, nearly 70% of residents have a high housing burden. Overcrowding is also a rampant issue, affecting over one in five households. Undocumented New Yorkers not eligible for federal housing assistance like Housing Choice Vouchers, Section 8 Project-based Rental Assistance or Public Housing. Access to NYC shelters are available to New Yorkers, regardless of immigration status.

Community Housing Assistance: Corona Mutual Aid has provided rent relief to undocumented residents.

HEALTH

In Elmhurst and Corona, over 1 in 5 residents is uninsured. Undocumented adults in NYC are ineligible for Medicaid or Medicare, though exceptions exist for those who are pregnant or seeking emergency services. All New York children are eligible for Child Health Plus, regardless of immigration status. NYC also offers limited health services through NYC CARE, regardless of immigration status.

Community Health Alternatives: Elmhurst Hospital | New Life Community Health Center | Plaza del Sol Family Health Center | Community Healthcare Network | All available regardless of immigration status.
